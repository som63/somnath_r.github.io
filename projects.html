Ongoing Research Projects

1. Synchronization in Classical and Quantum Networks

Illustration of collective behavior in classical (left) and quantum (right) networks, highlighting synchronization, coupling, and emergent dynamics.

This project focuses on understanding collective dynamical behaviors in classical and quantum oscillator networks. The primary aim is to analyze synchronization transitions, amplitude death, and collective resonance phenomena arising from nonlinear coupling, delay, and external driving.

Special emphasis is placed on quantum oscillator ensembles, where control of quantum fluctuations and decoherence is crucial for applications in quantum technologies and quantum information processing.

Collaborators:
Prof. S. Gupta (IIT Madras)
Prof. A. Roy Chowdhury (Jadavpur University)

2. Flow-Induced Vibration in Nonlinear Systems

This project investigates flow-induced vibrations (FIV) in nonlinear fluid–structure interaction systems, which are commonly encountered in aerospace, mechanical, and civil engineering applications.

The research aims to understand the onset of nonlinear instabilities, resonance, and chaotic oscillations due to fluid forcing, and to develop control strategies to suppress undesirable vibrations.

Advanced nonlinear analysis techniques are employed to study bifurcation structures and stability boundaries of the coupled fluid–structure systems.

Collaborator:
Prof. Sunetra Sarkar (Department of Aerospace Engineering, IIT Madras)

3. Nonlinear Energy Sinks and Targeted Energy Transfer

This project explores targeted energy transfer (TET) mechanisms using nonlinear energy sinks (NES) for vibration mitigation in mechanical systems.

The focus is on understanding energy localization, irreversible energy transfer, and dissipation in strongly nonlinear oscillatory systems under transient and periodic excitations.

Applications include vibration control in engineering structures and energy management in nonlinear lattices and oscillator chains.

Collaborators:
Prof. M. Coccolo
Prof. M. A. F. Sanjuán (Universidad Rey Juan Carlos, Spain)

4. Quantum Nonlinear Oscillators and Fluctuation Control: Applications in Quantum Computing

This project investigates fast-driven quantum nonlinear oscillators with the goal of controlling quantum fluctuations, noise-induced heating, and decoherence effects.

The study combines nonlinear dynamics, quantum control theory, and statistical physics to understand how external modulation can be used to engineer effective quantum states.

Potential applications include quantum computing architectures, quantum sensing, and quantum-enhanced information processing.

Collaborators:
Dr. C. Bhadra (Universidad Complutense de Madrid)
Prof. A. Zaccone (University of Milan)
